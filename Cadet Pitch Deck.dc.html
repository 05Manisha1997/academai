The regulatory tailwind

Banning AI failed. Now the EU requires schools to teach it.

Since 2 February 2025, Article 4 of the EU AI Act obliges every organisation that deploys AI — schools included — to ensure its people have a sufficient level of AI literacy. There is no off-the-shelf way to deliver it. That is the gap Cadet fills.

Private by design

Zero data liability — by architecture, not policy

The practice agent is simulated and runs on the student's own device. There is no live model to send anything to — so there is nothing to store, leak, or regret.

🔒
Nothing to store

Prompts never reach a server. Refresh the page and they are gone — there is no database of what kids typed.

🛡️
Nothing to leak

No minors' personal data at rest. Data-minimisation is the default state, not a setting an admin has to switch on.

🤖
Nothing to ingest

No third-party model sees student input — until a school explicitly opts into the supervised live-model bridge.

AI Act · Art. 50

Competitors wire students straight into a live model and inherit all of its data questions. Cadet does not — and every learner is always told, up front, that they are talking to an AI.
